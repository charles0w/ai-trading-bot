# End-of-Day Recap — Tuesday, May 19, 2026

## Market Recap

Risk-off, third straight down day for the S&P 500. The story all day was the bond market: long-end Treasury yields broke out to multi-decade highs and dragged equities — especially rate-sensitive growth, small caps and consumer cyclicals — lower. Defensives (healthcare, telecom) were the only places to hide.

| Index | Close | % Change |
|---|---|---|
| S&P 500 (SPY) | 7,353.61 | -0.67% |
| Nasdaq Comp. (QQQ) | 25,870.71 | -0.84% |
| Dow Jones (DIA) | 49,363.88 | -0.65% (-322 pts) |
| Russell 2000 (IWM) | — | -1.33% |
| VIX | ~19 (est.) | higher on the down tape |

**Rates — the driver:** The 10Y yield rose ~6bp to roughly 4.66%; the 20Y (~5.19%) and 30Y (~5.17–5.18%) printed fresh highs not seen in nearly two decades. The 10Y, 20Y and 30Y all set 52-week highs the same day. Sticky-inflation worries and chatter about the Fed potentially having to *hike* rather than cut kept pressure on duration.

**Sectors:** Tech and consumer discretionary (cyclicals) led the index lower. Healthcare and telecom led the gains — classic defensive rotation in a yields-up tape.

**Notable single-stock moves:** Gainers skewed defensive — Verizon ~+2.2%, Amgen ~+2.0%, Merck ~+1.5%. Decliners were tech/industrial — Cisco ~-3.0%, Boeing ~-2.6%, 3M ~-2.1%. Home Depot (HD) beat on EPS ($3.43 vs. $3.41) and revenue ($41.77B) and reaffirmed FY guidance, but same-store sales rose only 0.6% (vs. 0.9% est.); the stock slipped modestly (~-1.5%, recent print near $300) — a mild sell-the-news.

**Headlines that drove the tape:** the long-end yield breakout (30Y near a two-decade high); inflation/Fed rate-path anxiety; firmer oil earlier that faded; and conflicting Iran war headlines keeping a geopolitical risk premium in place. All eyes are now on Nvidia, which reports Wednesday after the close.
